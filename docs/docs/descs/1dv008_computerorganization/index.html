1DV008 - Computer organization (7.5 hec)

Prerequisites

1FY002, 1DV001, 1DV003, 1DV004.

Learnings outcomes

After completing the course the student is expected to:

1. Knowledge and understanding

  1. Understand the role of abstraction in the design of digital systems.
  2. Explain the major software and hardware abstractions in contemporary computer systems.
  3. Understand the operation of a moderately complex digital system.

2. Skills and abilities

  1. Analyze the performance of digital systems using measures such as latency and throughput.
  2. Design simple hardware systems based on a variety of digital abstractions such as ROMs and logic arrays, logic trees, state machines, pipelining, and buses.
  3. Analyze the operation of page-based virtual memory systems.
  4. Translate simple programs from a high-level language to machine language.

3. Judgement and approach

  1. Assess the impact of an architectural technique on the performance of application software.

Course contents

  • Digital circuits and CMOS
  • Combinational and Sequential logic
  • Finite state machines
  • Models of computation
  • ISA and Assembly language
  • The memory hierarchy
  • Pipelines
  • Virtual memory
  • Devices and interrupts
